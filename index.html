About

My work focuses on the foundations of large language models: data, evaluation, and post-training. I enjoy starting new research directions and building the systems to support them.

My career in LLM research began with a 20% project at Google Brain where I helped train Google Brain's first large-scale autoregressive language model. I have been a Research Engineer at Google Brain, a founding team member at Inflection, and a Principal Member of Technical Staff at Microsoft AI.

Since October 2024 I have been on sabbatical. I have been traveling, staying at a forest monastery in Thailand where I was temporarily ordained as a monk. I have also been working on side projects, practicing meditation, and learning Thai.

Selected Work

  • Instruction tuning and FLAN

    I started the FLAN project at Google, which introduced instruction tuning, and co-led the first academic publication on the concept. I built the initial model and organized the research effort to show that supervised fine-tuning on instruction-formatted datasets improves performance on unseen tasks. Instruction tuning is now a standard first step in modern LLM post-training.

  • Reasoning and Chain-of-Thought

    Co-author of "Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in Large Language Models." We showed that breaking problems into explicit steps improves accuracy on difficult tasks. This work helped establish methods used in reasoning-focused models and in scaling inference-time compute. arXiv

  • Code generation evaluation

    I created the Mostly Basic Python Problems (MBPP) dataset, released in August 2021, an early benchmark that researchers widely use to evaluate Python program synthesis, contemporaneous with HumanEval.

  • Foundational model building at Google and Inflection

    At Google Brain I created pretraining data pipelines, ran data-quality experiments, and set up evaluation systems for LaMDA, GLaM, and PaLM. I was a core contributor to PaLM. At Inflection I designed and ran pretraining, post-training, and safety experiments, and built supporting pipelines for pretraining and evaluation that enabled us to go from zero to competitive models such as Inflection-2.

See Google Scholar for a complete list of publications.
